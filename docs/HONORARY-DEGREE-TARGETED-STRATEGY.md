## The Right Targeting Frame

NOT: "people who want an easy path to a title"
YES: "people who have done the work and never got recognized"

These are different people and the distinction matters:

The wrong candidate:
Someone who wants credentials they have not earned
to deceive employers or clients about their qualifications.
We do not serve that person. That is not PICU's mission.

The right candidate:
Someone who has genuinely served — 15, 20, 30 years —
in ministry, counseling, community leadership, or music.
They did the work. They never had time or access to get
the formal credential. They deserve to be seen.
PICU sees them.

That is Dr. Poinsette's mission exactly as she stated it:
"leaders whose service and Kingdom impact often go
unrecognized or financially unsupported."

## The Outreach Sequence That Works

### Step 1: Recognition First, Offer Second
Do not lead with "apply for an honorary doctorate."
Lead with specific recognition of their work.

"I have been following your ministry for [X] months.
The work you have done with [specific population]
over [X] years is extraordinary. PICU recognizes
leaders who have demonstrated exactly this kind
of Kingdom impact."

### Step 2: Peer-to-Peer from Dr. Poinsette
The outreach converts best when it comes from her directly.
She is the Survivor-to-Servant archetype herself.
When she reaches out to another survivor-turned-servant,
it is recognition from someone who lived the same story.

### Step 3: Tell Them Exactly What It Is
"This is an honorary recognition — not an earned degree.
It is PICU's formal acknowledgment that your service
has been doctoral-level in its depth and impact.
You may use the title in ministry and community contexts."

Transparency here is not a liability. It builds trust.
The right candidate does not want to be deceived.
They want to be seen — honestly.

### Step 4: Show Them Lady J
The announcement is already published.
Lady J is a real person with a real story.
"This is what it looks like" converts better than
any description of the program.
