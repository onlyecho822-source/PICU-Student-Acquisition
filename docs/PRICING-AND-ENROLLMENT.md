## WHAT THE $2,500 INCLUDES

Every honoree receives the complete PICU Honorary Doctorate package:

✅  Official PICU Honorary Doctorate diploma with university seal
✅  Full academic regalia — cap, gown, and doctoral stole
✅  Formal conferment at PICU's annual commencement ceremony
✅  Permanent recognition in PICU's Hall of Honor
✅  Official announcement published through PICU channels
✅  Press release distributed to honoree's local media
✅  Professional ceremony photography
✅  "Nominating Fellow" program — honoree nominates the next class
✅  Legacy scholarship eligibility (3+ referrals = scholarship in their name)

## THE ENROLLMENT PROCESS

### Step 1 — Nomination
Dr. Poinsette personally reviews every nomination.
Nominations come from:
- Existing honorees (Friends & Family referral program)
- Direct outreach from Dr. Poinsette
- Self-nomination through the PICU website

### Step 2 — Approval
Dr. Poinsette reviews the nominee's life work, service record,
and fit with PICU's honorary degree criteria.
Decision communicated within 5 business days.

### Step 3 — Acceptance & Deposit
Upon approval, nominee receives an official PICU acceptance letter.
A **$500 non-refundable deposit** is due within 14 days to reserve
their seat in the upcoming ceremony class.

> The $500 deposit is non-refundable because it covers:
> regalia ordering, diploma preparation, ceremony materials,
> and the honoree's listing in the ceremony program.

### Step 4 — Balance Payment
The remaining **$2,000 balance** is due no later than
**30 days before the ceremony date**.

Payment plans available upon request — contact Dr. Poinsette directly.

### Step 5 — Ceremony
Honoree attends the PICU commencement ceremony.
Receives full academic regalia, official diploma, and formal recognition.
Becomes a PICU Founding Fellow or Class Fellow permanently.

### Step 6 — Legacy
Post-ceremony, honoree is invited to nominate their own candidates
for the next class through the Friends & Family Referral Program.

## OUTREACH MESSAGING — THE PRICE CONVERSATION

The price is never the first conversation. The recognition is.

**Wrong approach:**
"Our honorary doctorate costs $2,500..."

**Right approach:**
"We want to honor your 20 years of service. 
The ceremony includes your official doctoral regalia, 
your diploma, a press release to your local media, 
and permanent recognition in PICU's Hall of Honor.
To reserve your place in the ceremony, we ask for
a $500 enrollment deposit — and the investment
that covers everything is $2,500 total."

**When they ask about the price:**
"The full investment is $2,500, which covers your regalia,
diploma, ceremony, press release, and Hall of Honor listing.
We ask for a $500 deposit to reserve your seat —
that's the only non-refundable portion.
The balance of $2,000 is due 30 days before the ceremony,
and we do have payment plans available if that's helpful."

**When they hesitate:**
"I completely understand. Let me send you the full details
of what's included, and you can look at Lady J's ceremony
announcement to see exactly what this looks like.
There's no pressure — when you're ready, I'm here."
