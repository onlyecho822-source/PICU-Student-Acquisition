## THE FIVE CANDIDATE PROFILES (WHO TO FIND)

When searching, look for people who match one of these five profiles.
These are ranked by conversion rate — start with Profile A.

### PROFILE A: SURVIVOR-TO-SERVANT (Highest priority — 10/10 fit)
Someone who survived hardship and built a ministry from it.
Abuse, addiction, cancer, incarceration, homelessness —
they overcame it AND built something to help others do the same.
Example: Lady J. Example: Shuntell Alston.

Signals to look for:
- "My story led me to..." or "After surviving..."
- Founded a nonprofit or coaching program after personal trauma
- Speaks about their hardship publicly
- Has been serving others for 10+ years from that experience

### PROFILE B: WOMEN'S MINISTRY LEADER (10/10 fit)
Women who lead divorce recovery, single mothers' groups,
women's healing retreats, or First Lady ministry.
Dr. Poinsette's exact demographic — peer-to-peer outreach.

Signals: "Women's ministry" "divorce recovery" "kingdom woman"
"First Lady" "women's empowerment" in bio or profile

### PROFILE C: LONG-SERVING COMMUNITY PASTOR (9/10 fit)
Pastors, bivocational ministers, or chaplains with 15-30 years
of quiet, faithful service and no formal doctoral credential.
Hospitals, prisons, rural churches, small congregations.

Signals: "15 years of ministry" "founded [church] in [year] (decade ago)"
"chaplain" "bivocational" "serving since"

### PROFILE D: GOSPEL ARTIST / CREATIVE MINISTER (8/10 fit)
Gospel musicians, Christian speakers, faith podcasters with
30+ years of ministry through creative work.
Example: Calandra Gantt (30 years in gospel ministry).

Signals: Multiple awards, "ministry through music," licensed minister,
long-term faith-based content creation

### PROFILE E: CHRISTIAN ENTREPRENEUR / NONPROFIT FOUNDER (8/10 fit)
People who founded faith-based businesses, nonprofits, or
community organizations using Christian principles.

Signals: "Kingdom entrepreneur" "faith-based nonprofit" "founded in [year decade ago]"
