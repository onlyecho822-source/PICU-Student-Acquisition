# PICU PRICE ANCHOR — THE COMPARISON CONVERSATION
**Purpose:** Give every person who handles enrollment the
language to make $2,500 feel like the obvious choice.
**Updated:** 2026-03-29T21:47:34Z

---

## THE HONEST CONTEXT

$2,500 by itself feels large.
$2,500 compared to the alternatives feels like exactly what it is:
a highly accessible, fully legitimate recognition of a lifetime of work.

---

## THE COMPARISON TABLE

| Path to Doctoral Recognition | Cost | Time | What You Get |
|-------------------------------|------|------|--------------|
| Doctor of Ministry (DMin) | $30,000–$60,000 | 3–5 years | Earned degree |
| PhD in Theology | $40,000–$80,000 | 5–8 years | Earned degree |
| Christian conference registration | $500–$800 | 1 weekend | Certificate of attendance |
| Honorary Doctorate from PICU | **$2,500** | One ceremony | Diploma, regalia, press release, Hall of Honor, lifetime recognition |

---

## HOW TO USE THIS IN CONVERSATION

**Never lead with the comparison.** Always lead with recognition.
Only bring in the comparison if they hesitate on price.

**If they say "that seems like a lot":**

"I completely understand. Here is some honest context —
a traditional doctoral program costs $30,000 to $60,000
and takes years of coursework. What PICU is doing is
formally recognizing the doctorate you have already earned
through decades of real-world ministry and service.
The $2,500 covers your regalia, your official diploma,
the ceremony, a press release to your local media,
and permanent recognition in our Hall of Honor.
That is the full picture.
And if the timing is challenging, we do have payment plans."

**If they say "I need to think about it":**

"Of course. Take the time you need.
I will send you the Lady J announcement so you can see
exactly what this looks like — the ceremony, the regalia,
the recognition. That will give you everything you need
to make your decision.
There is no pressure. When you are ready, I am here."

**If they say "I can't afford it right now":**

"I appreciate your honesty. We have a payment plan option —
a $500 deposit to reserve your seat, then monthly payments
leading up to the ceremony. Let me send you the details
and you can see if that works for your situation."

---

## THE THREE-SENTENCE SUMMARY

Use this when someone asks "what is the cost" cold:

"The full investment is $2,500 — that is your regalia,
diploma, ceremony, press release, and Hall of Honor listing.
We ask for a $500 deposit to reserve your seat,
and the balance is due 30 days before the ceremony.
Payment plans are available."

Then stop talking. Let them respond.
