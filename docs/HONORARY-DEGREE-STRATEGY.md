## The Honorary Degree Opportunity

An honorary degree from PICU is fundamentally different from
an earned degree. It is recognition. Prestige. A credential
that says: "This institution, founded by Dr. Poinsette,
sees your life's work and names it doctoral-level."

The person who wants an honorary degree is not a student.
They are a leader who has already done the work.
They want the title. The wall plaque. The ceremony.
The ability to write "Doctor" before their name
in a context that honors their ministry and mission.

This is a completely different acquisition motion than
finding doctoral candidates who want to earn a degree.

---

## Who Wants an Honorary Degree

**The exact profile:**

```
Age:          45-70
Background:   Ministry, faith-based entrepreneurship,
              community leadership, nonprofit leadership,
              gospel music, Christian media, evangelism
Achievement:  20+ years of service OR notable impact
              in their community or field
Motivation:   Recognition, legacy, credibility,
              ability to use "Dr." title,
              ceremony and celebration of life's work
Income:       $60,000-$250,000+
Pain point:   Never had time or access to earn a degree
              formally but has done doctoral-level work
              in the real world
```

**The five clearest candidate types:**

### 1. The Seasoned Pastor / Bishop
30+ years in ministry. Built churches. Raised congregations.
Never got the formal degree. Peers have "Dr." before their name.
Wants the recognition. Willing to pay for legitimate credentials.
**Where to find them:** Bishop/Elder networks, denominational
conferences, large church Facebook pages, gospel conventions.

### 2. The Faith-Based Entrepreneur
Built a business on Christian principles. Speaks at conferences.
Has a platform. Wants academic credibility to match.
Dr. Lesley Poinsette is herself this profile.
**Where to find them:** Christian business networks, Faith-based
entrepreneur Facebook groups, Christian Chambers of Commerce.

### 3. The Gospel Artist / Christian Media Personality
Recorded albums. Built a following. Served the community
through music and media. Wants a credential that honors
the spiritual work behind the artistry.
**Where to find them:** Gospel music Facebook groups,
Christian radio personalities, YouTube Christian creators.

### 4. The Community Champion
Ran nonprofits. Served neighborhoods. Built programs
that changed lives. Never got paid much. Never got a degree.
Deserves recognition.
**Where to find them:** Community organization networks,
faith-based nonprofit directories, local church leaders.

### 5. The Ministry Spouse / First Lady
Behind every senior pastor is often a partner who built
as much as they did. Overlooked. Underrecognized.
The honorary degree says: "We see your work too."
**Where to find them:** First Ladies conferences,
Women in Ministry networks, church leadership groups.

## The Pricing Question

Honorary degrees need a clear price point that:
- Respects the dignity of the recipient
- Reflects the value of the credential
- Is accessible to ministry leaders (not just wealthy)
- Generates sustainable revenue

**Suggested tiers:**

| Tier | Price | Includes |
|------|-------|---------|
| Honorary Doctorate | $1,500 | Certificate, credentials, recognition |
| Honorary Doctorate + Ceremony | $2,500 | Above + virtual ceremony, video |
| Presidential Honorary | $5,000 | Above + featured in PICU publications, speaking invitation |

At 10 recipients/month at $1,500 average = $15,000/month passive revenue.
At 20 recipients/month = $30,000/month.
