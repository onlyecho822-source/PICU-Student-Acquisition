## The Core Demographic Truth

The ideal PICU honoree is not looking for PICU. They do not know honorary recognition at this level is available to them. They have spent years or decades serving others — in ministry, healthcare, education, community work, or advocacy — and have received little or no formal acknowledgment. When we find them and say "You did the work. We see it." — that message lands with extraordinary force.

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Age | 45–65 |
| Gender | Any (women slightly overrepresented in this profile) |
| Location | Southeast US, urban and suburban |
| Role | Pastor, minister, evangelist, women's ministry leader, church administrator |

## Disqualifying Signals

These signals indicate the wrong candidate. Do not pursue:

| Signal | Reason |
|--------|--------|
| Actively seeking an earned degree | Wrong product; they want enrollment, not recognition |
| Under 35 years old | Insufficient service history for honorary recognition |
| No demonstrated service record | Honorary recognition requires evidence of work done |
| Primarily motivated by credential for career advancement | Honorary doctorate does not confer academic standing; wrong motivation |
| Hostile to faith-based institutions | Product is explicitly faith-based; misalignment will create friction |
